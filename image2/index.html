The founder of the Old Kingdom was King Djoser, and its capital was in the city of Memphis. Among the famous kings who ruled Egypt during this period were Sneferu, Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure.

Elephantine is 1,200 metres (3,900 ft) from north to south, and is 400 metres (1,300 ft) across at its widest point. The layout of this and other nearby islands in Aswan can be seen from west bank hillsides along the Nile.

The Egyptian name for Thebes was wꜣs.t, "City of the wꜣs", the sceptre of the pharaohs, a long staff with an animal's head and a forked base. From the end of the New Kingdom, Thebes was known in Egyptian as njw. t-jmn, the "City of Amun"
